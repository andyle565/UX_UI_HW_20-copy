Sara Sanctuary Responsive Web Design

Project Summary:

SARA Sanctuary stands for Society For Animal Rescue and Adoption. The non-profit cares and feeds over 600 animals. The website has over 6,000 visitors a month and many of the users do not feel comfortable making a donation on the website.

Goal:

Redesign the website for the users to feel trustworthy to make a donation to the non-profit.

My Role and Responsibilities:

Proto Persona User Persona User Interviews Affinity Diagram Problem Statement User Insight Wireframes Prototypes

Timeline

2 weeks

User Research

Problem statement

SARA Sanctuary website was designed to allow visitors to build trust with the organization and donate to the cause. We have observed that SARA’s website donation process does not flow smoothly and does not build trust with the visitors, which is causing many visitors to leave the site and not donate to the non-profit. How might we improve SARA’s website so that we can increase the number of donations?

Proto Persona

I created a Photo Persona, who will resemble someone that would visit SARA’s website. Proto Persona is a great way to start the project to determine the target audience.

Interview Plan

User interviews are helpful to collect and synthesize information in order to create empathy with the users.

Objectives

To find out what SARA Sanctuary’s branding is. To understand SARA Sanctuary’s current audience and who they want for the future. TO find out their main priorities and what they would like to change.

Insights

After interviewing with the stakeholders, there are over 6000+ visitors a month, some visitors were determined to donate that they email because the process was complicated. Stakeholders want to tone to remain positive and impactful. Their main priorities are donations, volunteers, and adoption.

Survey

My team and I posted a survey on Facebook and received a total of 17 responses. The survey’s intention is to determine if the participants supported any non-profit organization, why they supported the organizations, and find out what frustrations they currently have With their organizations.

The main things that stood out to us is that majority of our interviewees wanted to know the organization’s mission statement and where the organizations are putting their money into. The frustrations we’ve discovered is that many of their non-profit organizations are not up to date with their website design, therefore they have difficultly navigating through the site.

Definition & Synthesis

Affinity Diagram

After conducting the interviews, we created an Affinity Diagram for a brainstorming session with the team. Affinity Diagram is a useful process to sort and identify the insights in data. We made our data come to live by Inputing Point of View Statements, such as “I want the mission statement to be clear” and “I want the navigation to be simple to use” in order to have a better understanding with our users and empathize with them.

User Persona

I created a User Persona for the team, which is based on all of the research we have done. The purpose of a User Persona is to create realistic representations of our key users. Our User Persona, Emily, has been volunteering and participating in many local philanthropy events and her main goal os to make a donation to an animal shelter.

User Insight

I constructed an User Insight to help clarify and develop a deep understanding of a User Persona by telling their story. User insights is useful to uncover underlying motivations, analyst unmet needs, validate ideas and help come up with a solution for the problems later in the process. Emily Patterson, who is an animal advocate, wants to donate to SARA’s Sanctuary because she wants to support her local community and values the life of every animal. During our interviews, we discovered that the majority of interviewees felt uninformed and insecure about the donation process. Therefore, we believe that by creating a more clear, trustworthy donation page and web-pretense might increase the amount of donations and user return to the website. We might do this by redesigning the information artitecture, the visual hierarchy, the navigation system, and making the donation information more clear to our visitors. Doing this will allow our website to be trustworthy to donate and increase traffic of visitors.

User Journey Map

The team created a User Journey Map to show a visual representation of the experience of the user. Specifically, it is helpful to identify where in the process the users are experiencing the pain points. The opportunities section will show how to resolve the pain points. According to our User Journey Map, Emily does not exactly understand what SARA’s Sanctuary is all about, therefore including a mission statement on the homepage will help Emily have a better understanding of the purpose of SARA’s Sanctuary. Simplifying the options of donations will be intended to reduce the amount of cognitive overload Emily will experience when donating.

User Flow

I helped out the team to create a User Flow to determine how the User will interact with the website. The “happy path” is for our Users to make a monthly donation, since the stakeholder stated in the interviews that they heavily rely on monthly donations to determine the amount of charities they will receive for the sanctuary.

Card Sorting

I helped the team during the Card Sorting exercise to design the structure and determine the natural grouping of the information architecture. Card Sorting is useful during the redesign of a website naviation, where designers will collaborate and organize the categories to help it make sense. We worked on recategorizing the navigation to build trust into the site. The new navigation will consist of About, Donate, Volunteer, Adoption, and Contact Us tabs

Low/Mid Fidelity Wireframes

Homepage - Followed the L.A.T.C.H Principles, we placed the mission statement at the top of the visual herarchy to show the purpose of SARA.

Donations - At the top of the visual hierarchy, the purpose of the donation will be placed. followed by Monthly donation and One-Time donation. Monthly donation is located at the top because the Stakeholders rely heavily on their monthly donations.

Breadcrumbs were added to show the location of the page. The order of steps is included to show the users the visibility of system status, which is one of Jakob Nielson’s 10 usability heuristic for UI design.

Due to the Covid pandemic, my team and I worked remotely to develop our wireframes. Here is a screenshot of my awesome teammates and I working on Figma.

Usability Testing

Tasks

1. Find the Donation page 2. Make a $100 donation 3. View the side-scrolling articles of “See What We Are Up To”

Results

Majority of users were able to complete all of the tasks. 1 user had trouble finding the “See What We Are Up To” section. Buttons will need to be all of the same shape. Breadcrumbs will need to move up above the heading due to visual hierarchy. Will need to make a call-to-action on The Thank You Page.

Usability testing were conducted via Zoom.

High Fidelity Wireframes

Homepage - The mission statement is located at the top of the visual hierarchy in order to show the purpose of SARA and help build trust with the users.

Monthly donations page - Iterated the text bigger for users too easily read. Moved the breadcrumbs above the header. Added shadow around the card to show the visual cue for the user.

Added a call-to-action on the Thank You page to help users stay engaged with the website.

The mobile wireframes were made responsive to improve the usability of the web pages by using flexible grids and images to apply condition style sheets. One a desktop version, we used 12 column grids on our prototypes and on the mobile version we used 4 column grids on our prototypes.

Conclusion

Our solution tested successful, were our users stated they felt comfortable of making a donation and the process felt smooth to them. When users do not have to think hard to navigate through a site, it is most likely a win for the designers. Looking back after the project, I believe our group could have used the “I like, I Wish, and What If Method during our brainstorming of UI ideas. There were many times through the design phase where we would speak out on the ideas that we liked. If we would have used that method, then our design process would have been more effect and get the right designs down. The next step is to create animations on the web page for the users to have more interactions with the site. We are still in contact with the stakeholders and are in talks to implement the designs into their site.
